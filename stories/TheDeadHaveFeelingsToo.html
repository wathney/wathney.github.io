The Dead Have Feelings Too


By Lars I. Wathney


Richard woke up with a headache, and a stiff neck. He was also dead. He was lying in a ditch, by the side of the road, with the right side of his neck eaten away. He could feel the wound, but it did not register as pain normally does. He felt little discomfort, but was simply aware that part of his neck was missing. He turned his head side to side, and worked his jaw a few times. The cords in his neck could be seen expanding, and contracting as he moved. At least his mouth worked, which was good news, because the only sensation causing any real discomfort was a gnawing hunger in the pit of his belly. As a member of the living, Richard had possessed all manner of fears, goals, motivations, and desires. Now, his sole purpose in non-life had been focused into one incredibly bright pin-prick inside his poorly functioning brain: Feed.


Richard stood up on wobbly legs, trying to keep his balance, as his body acclimated to a new state of existence. A sound in the distance caught his ear, a dull whine. He wheeled around, and saw down the road, a car approaching. Richard didn't remember exactly what cars were, but had a vague impression of their importance, and that, combined with the fact that it was moving, led him to believe that it might be something good to eat. He raised his arms in the air, with his fingers stretched towards the approaching car, and a low, guttural moan escaped his pale blue lips. It just felt right. As the car grew closer, Richard lurched towards it. With a roar, it passed, swerving slightly to avoid hitting him as it did so. The edge of the windshield caught his right hand, and sent him spinning around, to collapse on the asphalt.


Again he stood, and this time the pinky and ring finger of his right hand were bent back at an odd angle, but he did not seem to notice. In the field next to the road, Richard saw a horse with its head down, cropping the grass. It raised it's head to look at Richard, still chewing. Richard turned and stumbled towards the horse. It stopped chewing. Richard reached the barbed wire fence bordering the field, and flipped, head over heels, onto the other side. As he picked himself back up, the horse whinnied nervously. As Richard approached, the horse's eyes rolled wild in their sockets, and it turned to canter off towards the barn in the distance. Undaunted, Richard trudged after it. The hunger inside him was growing stronger, and more insistent, he would not give up a hot meal that easily. The field through which he walked was filled with patches of green grass, still wet with morning dew, and soon his shoes were soaked, yet on he walked. His cold feet, and the wet socks squishing in his shoes, were ignored by Richard. He was solely focused on his ever-increasing hunger. He began to chew on his own lips as he followed the horse across the field. As he approached the barn, Richard could see two young girls off to his left, playing on the other side of the fence, in front of a house. Forgetting the horse, he turned towards the girls, approached the fence, and with a decided lack of coordination, climbed over. He collapsed on the other side, and finally caught the attention of the girls, who had been lost in a fantasy land where dolls have jobs, and clean houses, and makes dinner, and no one is ever sad, except for maybe the dog, because he doesn't get any table scraps. Feeding it will not make it go away, it only encourages them.


“Are you okay, Mister?” one of the girls asked.


Her name was Elizabeth Bennet, because her mother liked Jane Austen, and her sister's name was Annabel Lee, because her father liked Edgar Allan Poe. They lived here, in this house, on this farm, with their mother and father, who loved them very much.


As he tried to right himself, Richard resembled a man who has just exited a bar after having too much to drink, and after collapsing outside, is attempting to stand back up. After Richard hauled himself up, Elizabeth could see his mouth was bloody, where he had chewed away his own lips. She screamed, and Richard lunged towards her. Her scream turned into a gurgle as Richard bit into her windpipe and pulled it out with his teeth. Her blood ran down his chin, as he chewed with his face held up in the air. The relief which flowed through his body was unlike anything he had ever felt as a living person. Sex paled in comparison. The pleasure he had felt at a party in college, slamming shots of tequila, and doing lines of blow off a hooker's ass did not even begin to approach it. The birth of his own daughter was like the ghost of a long-forgotten memory. Elizabeth's arms and legs flailed around as Richard held her down, and ate her face. Her sister Annabel ran towards the house screaming bloody murder, and waving her arms over her head. Ever the glutton, Richard left Elizabeth's cooling body in the dirt, and staggered after Annabel.


He had almost caught up to her by the time she reached the front door, and as she closed it behind her, Richard slammed against the outside, throwing the door open, and sending Annabel sprawling onto her back. Richard stalked towards Annabel, as she crawled away screaming, ending up under the kitchen table. Carolyn had heard her daughter screaming, and came running downstairs, to find her hiding under the table, as Richard threw chair's out of the way, and tried to grab her. Without a thought for her own safety, Carolyn launched herself at Richard, climbing onto his back. She wrapped one arm around his neck, and with her other hand, pulled his head back in the air. Richard gnashed his teeth, and spun around in circles, trying to throw Carolyn off. While Richard was distracted, Annabel crawled out from under the table, ran upstairs, and locked herself in the bathroom. Meanwhile, Richard slammed Carolyn's back against the wall, and with a grunt of exhaled air, her grip loosened. Richard pulled free the arm around his neck, and sank his teeth into the meat of her bicep. Carolyn fell to the ground, clutching her arm where Richard had bitten off a chunk. She tried to cry out, but the wind had been knocked out of her. Richard chewed on the piece of bicep, and turned to face Carolyn as she sunk to the floor. She saw his lipless face, the blood that covered his mouth, and stained the front of his shirt, and she managed a thin wail as Richard fell upon her.


He stripped her bones of flesh like a basket of chicken wings, devoured her internal organs, then cracked her skull open and scooped out the brains. This one wouldn't be coming back. His stomach distended with Carolyn's flesh, Richard's hunger was satiated for the moment. He rose from the stripped carcass, and his body swayed side-to-side as he waddled towards the open front door, and secreted himself away in the corner behind it. He fell asleep standing there, and while his meal settled, Annabel was curled up in the bathtub crying. After a couple hours, the bathroom door opened a crack, and Annabel peeked out. She had heard her mother screaming earlier, but the house was quiet now. She didn't see anything down the hall, but then she couldn't see downstairs. She slowly pushed the door open, and crept out on her tip-toes, looking back and forth, still seeing nothing. She moved towards the stairs, and descended, crouching as she did so. From the living room where she stood, her mother's body was hidden, where it lay in the kitchen. Scanning the room, she didn't see Richard, and so approached the kitchen door. Standing in the doorway, she saw what remained of her mother, and the cry that escaped her lips would have broken the heart of anyone with an ounce of emotion. From where he nodded behind the door, Richard heard her cry, and came bursting out. He was on her before she could react, and as she cried for her mother and her sister, Richard devoured Annabel's little body.


As Douglas Mackenzie rolled down the long gravel drive which led to his house, he could already tell something was wrong. On his way home, he had seen three different car accidents on the side of the road, as well as an increased police presence in town. He had also heard a strange news report on the radio, about people acting violent, perhaps as a result of some new drug. It seemed like every month, there was a new designer drug hitting the market, many ended up having horrible side-effects. Whatever happened to the classics? Doug pulled up in front of his house, and saw his daughter's body lying on the ground, surrounded by a pool of blood soaking into the dirt. He kicked his car door open and sprinted towards Elizabeth. As he approached her body, he knelt down beside her.


“Oh God, oh God, oh God, what did the do to you baby?” he moaned, wringing his hands, his face twisted in pain, as tears streamed down his cheeks.


He gently cradled Elizabeth's lifeless body against his chest, and with his head lowered, rocked forward and back, as though lulling her to sleep. He wept as he had never wept before in his life, and muttered semi-coherent sentence fragments that included words like “why”, and “God”, and “love” and “fair”. He rose, carrying her limp body towards the house. As he approached, he saw the front door left open, swinging back and forth in the breeze. He also saw the trail of blood leading out of the house. Waves of panic washed over him and he entered, and his worst fears were realized. The bodies of Annabel and Carolyn were mangled beyond recognition. Their flesh had been stripped from their skeletons, and the skulls crushed. Richard had eaten their brains, and dug out their eyes, squishing them between his molars, where the vitreous humor squirted down his throat like a fruit snack. Richard had even cracked open some of their bones, in order to suck out the marrow. Doug's legs were feeling weak, and so he gently laid Elizabeth's body on the couch and sat down on the floor. He sat for a moment, looking dazed, then flopped over on his side, and curled himself into a ball.


After several minutes of laying catatonic in the fetal position, Douglas was brought back to reality (or what currently passed for it), by a gurgling moan. His eyes focused, and he sat up, mumbling, “Wha …?”


Next to him, on the couch, Elizabeth moaned again. Her head turned, and her milky eyes focused on Doug.


“Oh, Honey …” he whispered, and he felt bad, but a numbness was spreading inside him, and he did not feel nearly as bad as a man watching his dead daughter re-animate, should.


Elizabeth sat up and swung her legs off the couch. Even though it was the body of his daughter, she looked like a monster. Her throat was torn out, and most of her face was missing, exposing the skull underneath, in many places. The whole front of her dress was stained with blood turning a rusty brown around the edges. While Doug knew that this was impossible, he was also no dummy, and he had seen zombie movies before. As he watched the body of his daughter standing up, he remembered being a teenager, and going to a midnight showing of Night of the Living Dead. After the movie, he was terrified as he walked to his car, looking over his shoulder, and checking the back seat before he got in. He stood now, on legs still wobbling, and backed away. Elizabeth rose from the couch, her legs uncertain as well, and took a step towards her father. As Doug continued to back-step, Elizabeth fell towards him. He reflexively swung his leg up, landing a kick to the stomach, which sent her sprawling. As Elizabeth struggled to get back up, Doug turned, and bounded upstairs to his room, and to his closet where he kept his hunting rifle. It was a .308 he used for hunting Blacktail Deer. When he came out of his room carrying the rifle, Elizabeth was climbing the stairs. With tears rolling down his cheeks, he centered her head in the cross-hairs, whispered, “I love you, honey”, and fired.


The bullet was designed for controlled expansion, and had a polymer tip, which gave it a flat trajectory. It flew true, and when it entered Elizabeth's skull, the polymer tip disintegrated, and the bullet started expanding. By the time it exited her head, the bullet had peeled back into the shape of a mushroom cap. It shattered the back of her skull, and took most of her brains with it, spraying them down the stairs. The jet of brains and blood shooting out, snapped her head forward. Her face slammed against the stairs, and then her body slid down, leaving a smeared trail of blood behind it. Douglas sad down on the floor, at the top of the stairs, and rested the rifle in his lap. He looked down at himself, and the blood staining him, from when he had carried Elizabeth inside. He had just found his family eaten, and then shot his own daughter in the head, and all he could think about was washing the blood off his hands.


Richard was wandering through the woods, feeling rather content, given his current state of being. After eating Carolyn and Annabel, he had decided to go for a walk to settle his stomach. While the hunger was ever-present, it had subsided in intensity after a good meal. It was quiet out here, amongst the trees. The only sound was the creaking of the Coast Redwoods, as they swayed in the breeze. It was hypnotizing, and Richard, lulled into a trance, wandered for hours. When he emerged into a clearing occupied by three campers sitting around a portable stove, he was stunned and pausing, looked at them, quizzical. The campers saw him, and the gore which covered his front, and started screaming. Richard's face twisted in a vicious snarl as he ran at the campers. One man turned on his heel and sprinted off into the woods, leaving the others to fend for themselves. A woman stood up, and stumbling backwards, tripped over the log she had been sitting on, and fell flat on her back. With the wind knocked out of her she, lay on the ground, wheezing for breath. Another man stood up, holding his hands out, as Richard came at him.


He might have been trying to fend Richard off, or perhaps it was an attempt to placate him, but whatever his intention, it was unsuccessful. Richard bit off the first two joints of the mans index and middle finger, on his left hand. As the man clutched his bleeding hand to his chest and backed away, Richard followed, catching the portable stove with his foot. The pot of water which sat atop, tipped over onto the legs of the woman laying on the ground. Her legs were clad in nothing but kacki shorts, and what little breath she had regained was used to scream, as the scalding water spilled across them. The stove spiraled through the air, and landed against a tent staked nearby. The Ripstop nylon of the tent caught, and went up in a blaze, which spread across the tent dripping molten plastic in its wake. The piercing cries of another woman echoed from inside the burning tent, and she burst out wearing nothing but panties and burning plastic which clung to her skin like napalm. She spun around, slapping at the flames, which only spread it further across her body. The man missing two fingers fled into the forest, and Richard followed, leaving the burning woman to collapse in a smoking, lifeless heap.


The man missing two fingers (his name was Andy), soon started feeling woozy as shock set in. He was clumsy, and stumbling, looking back to see how close Richard was, when a low hanging tree branch dragged across his left shoulder. For a split second it felt like a hand clutching him in it's steel grip, then he was falling. Andy hit the ground face-first, and moments later Richard caught up. He fell on Andy's back, reaching his hands around and digging them under the rib cage. Richard pulled Andy's body up against him as he chewed the meat off his neck. Richard was still full from his previous meals, and soon after starting on Andy's shoulders, turned and vomited on the ground. The gelatinous brown mass of partially-digested human flesh looked like rotten lutefisk. After purging himself, Richard went back to eating, and soon finished off the rest of Andy.


After Andy's screams had died away, and after he had finished eating, Richard could hear a booming in the distance. He stood up, and set off in the general direction the booming emanated from, as he reasoned there might be more to eat. He trudged on, with his rounded belly protruding in front of him. As he walked, the boom grew louder, and he could hear the barking of animals underneath. Not as tasty as humans, but food nonetheless. By now, Richard had gotten used to the rotting stench of his own body, and could barely sense it, himself. A Gust of wind brought the sounds reverberating louder, and carried with it, a hint of smell. It too, was the smell of rot and decay, and though faint, was distinctly different from Richards own. In a way, it was also fresh and invigorating. This new smell only stoked Richards hunger, and he redoubled his efforts at finding its source. He burst through a thicket of trees, onto a rocky beach, and found what he was searching for. It was the Pacific Ocean. Waves crashed against the rocks, churning the water in a frothy swirl before washing up on the sand. Nearby a pod of seals bleated, and flopped around on the rocks near the edge of the surf.

Richard eyed the seals for a second, before breaking into a quick trot towards them. As he approached, they dove into the sea. Their bodies, bloated and clumsy on land, became lithe and graceful in the water. Richard waded out after them, but they avoided him with ease. Soon he was up to his armpits, and the waves rocked him back and forth as they rolled around him. He could barely touch the seabed with his toes, and he struggled to keep purchase. He was trying to make his way back towards the shore, when he heard the deep, rumbling bellows of a fog horn. It was a fishing boat coming into port, heading for the dock in the distance, which was currently obscured by the billows of fog rolling off the sea. He could make out the silhouettes of men on the deck, and waded towards the fishing boat, but was soon treading water. He managed to stay afloat for a while, but as he neared the boat, his head began to bob underwater. A flood light snapped on, aimed at him, and he heard voices yelling from on deck, as he struggled not to go under. A life preserver hit the water nearby, and he lunged for it, managing to get an arm through. He was then able to wrap his other arm around it, and hang on while they hauled him out of the water. Several men pulled him over the railing, and Richard flopped onto his back, surrounded by a spreading pool of cold pacific ocean.


One of his rescuers knelt next to him exclaiming, “Jesus buddy, you look terrible! What happened to–”, but he didn't get a chance to finish his sentence, because he started screaming when Richard bit into his calf.


“The fucker bit me!” he hollered, as he staggered back, clutching his wounded leg.


Richard chewed on the piece of leg he had torn off, and used the railing to pull himself up. The sailors formed a wide semi-circle around him. At first too stunned to react, they were still hesitant, and tried to keep their distance while simultaneously closing in on him. Richard swayed back and forth, then lunged at the man directly in front of him, wrapping around his body like the spider embraces the fly.


Lazaro sat in the cab perched high atop the dock crane he operated, eating a bologna sandwich. It was his lunch break, but sometimes he spent them up here. He enjoyed his job. The heights didn't bother him, he was cool under the pressure of operating a massive, costly piece of machinery, and it was peaceful up here by himself. That was why he spent his lunch break up here. No annoying co-workers, no wife bitching at him, no screaming kids, just him and the birds. As he sat with his feet up, eating his sandwich, he watched the banks of fog drift slow across the water. Through one of them, he could see the shape of a fishing boat resolve. He could tell it was a fishing boat, because of the shape. It was a Trawler, designed not for speed, but to haul nets full of fish in the water behind it. He cracked open a can of soda, and took a couple sips. When he had worked down on the docks, he often had a beer with his lunch, as did many of the guys. If he had been caught drinking while operating the crane, however, he would be fired quicker than a speeding marlin. So now he drank soda. He took another sip, and as the fishing boat approached, he could make out the tiny shapes of men, running around like ants, on the deck. The boat grew closer, and it occurred to Lazaro that they were coming in kind of fast. It was then, that he saw some of the tiny ant-men jumping overboard. His feet dropped to the floor as he sat up in his seat, watching the boat grow closer still. Something was definitely wrong, it should be slowing down by now, and on the ground, some of the men had dropped what they were doing, and were pointing at the approaching vessel. Then they ran, as the boat reached the end of it's collision course.


Lazaro could hear the great rending of wood, as the bow made contact with the dock, and plowed through it. Beams of timber, and ragged shards of metal flew through the air, crushing shipping containers, and tearing apart fleeing men. As the boat slowed, waves of people spilled over the sides, some of them plunging into the water, and some landing on the dock with sickening thuds. Many of them, after what should have been a fatal fall, rose again, with twisted limbs and faces. They chased down the fleeing dock workers, and tore into their flesh with gnashing teeth. Swarms of them would pile over the wounded, pulling their bodies apart and devouring them like … ants. Lazaro sat watching in horror, and reached over to snap shut the door lock. It looked like he would be up here for a while. He wished he had a beer.


Richard tumbled over the side of the fishing boat onto a squirming pile of undead bodies, and rolled down onto the dock. Regaining his feet, Richard took in his surroundings. Packs of his fellow undead chased fleeing workers down the docks, closing on them, and pulling them to the ground. The gurgling screams of dying men wafted on the cool ocean breeze. Richard would approach packs of the feeding zombies, but each time they would turn their heads up to hiss and wail at him. Reaching the end of the dock, Richard followed the dirt trail which led back into the woods. The fog that floated between the trees, diffused the light, and he could not see the sun in the sky. It appeared the fog itself was glowing a pale blue-grey. Thick patches, and wispy tendrils drifting by entranced him.


A man named Darcy crouched behind a rock to the left of the trail, too afraid to fight, and too afraid to flee. Darcy's father had been one of those men always on the road, never settling in one place too long, never happy unless on the move. He gathered no moss, and had no time to raise a child. He was also a Johnny Cash fan, and so inspired by “A Boy Named Sue”, he had named his son Darcy. However, when Darcy was inevitably picked on, he did not fight back, or rise to the occasion, but sunk away from it. He became a meek, cowering mouse of a man. As Richard passed the rock behind which Darcy hid, Darcy started to panic. His heart was racing like a humming bird, and he broke out in hives, his body itching all over. He felt a tickle in his throat and tried to hold back a cough. His knees throbbed from kneeling, and dry twigs beneath his feet threatened to snap if he shifted his weight. Something was bound to give him away, and he knew it. That is the fate of his kind. He held his arm over his mouth, trying to muffle his cough, and felt a lump of phlegm spatter against his sleeve. On the trail, he heard Richard pause for a moment, then turn and approach his hiding place. Now he had no choice. Bracing his feet against the rock, he leapt up and ran into the woods. Richard issued a guttural moan and took off in pursuit. After two minutes of running at full speed through the forest, with branches whipping at his face, Darcy's lungs began to burn, and he felt a stitch developing in his side. He knew he could not continue at this pace for much longer, but still heard Richard not far behind. So when he ran into a small clearing with a tall tree in the center, he took his opportunity. Using the stubs of broken branches as hand-holds, and foot-holds, he managed to climb high enough so that he could grab the lowest intact branch, and pull himself up into the tree. As he hauled himself up, Richard came bursting into the clearing, and threw himself against the trunk of the tree. Leaping and snarling, he swung his arms above him, but the branches were too high to reach. When he realized he could not climb the tree he circled it three times, always looking up at Darcy, his jaw hanging open. Then he stopped and stood motionless, staring up.


Hours had passed, Richard didn't know how many. It was now dark, but it made no difference to him. He could stand here indefinitely, waiting for something to happen. Then something did. A, deer burst from the foliage to his right, bound across the clearing, and disappeared back into the woods. Richard broke into a run, following after the animal. The deer, however, was too quick for him, and Richard had soon lost track of it. He moaned half-heartedly after the deer, then turned and ambled back towards the tree in the clearing, where he knew there would be food waiting for him. When he reached it, Darcy was climbing down. He was at the lowest branch, about to drop to the ground. Richard ran towards him, jumping in the air and snatching at Darcy's dangling foot, But Darcy was too quick. Pulling his leg up, he scrambled back into the tree. But in his haste, his foot slipped when a patch of bark beneath it crumbled and sloughed off. He pitched forward, a limb cracking against the bridge of his nose. Bright silver bursts of pain as he clutched his wounded face, and then he was tumbling down through the branches.


Richard watched as Darcy hit the ground with a satisfying thud, and in moments Richard was on him. Chewing on Darcy's face, and cracking open his ribcage to get at the steaming organs. He reveled in the orgasm of flesh, and hot, salty blood. The meal, hard-earned, tasted that much better. His initial lust satiated, Richard sat picking at his meal, when a young woman stumbled into the clearing. She was dead too, but freshly turned, her body still in pretty good shape. And what a shape it was, Richard felt long-forgotten urges stirring in the ancient recesses of his mind. She approached the fresh corpse with caution, groaning and gurgling. Richard stepped back, inviting her to feed. She knelt but kept her eyes on Richard as her mouth tore a bite off of the arm. Richard drew closer, and she jumped, but kept eating. He crouched across from her, and they looked into each others eyes as they fed. When the body was stripped of everything edible, they stood, and circled the sad looking pile of bones, with eyes locked. Moaning and whining at each other, they moved closer, until Richard stepped over the body and grabbing her arms, pulled her body against his. He smelled her neck, and she moaned with pleasure. He could feel her grey nipples growing erect against his chest. It began to rain, the water washing away the blood. They rutted there in the mud, next to the remains of their meal, and Richard shot his diseased seed inside of her. When he awoke the next morning, she was gone. He looked around, and with a sighing moan wandered off in search of another meal.


Her name was Valerie, and she was walking through the forest looking for a place to nest. Deep in the woods, she happened upon the entrance to a small cave, between two great boulders. Getting down on her hands and knees, she crawled inside, and there on a bed of dry leaves, she slumbered as the baby grew inside her belly. By the time she emerged, her belly was distended, and lumps writhed beneath its skin. She fell on her back and moaned in pain, as the baby began to eat its way out. Like a chicken emerging from the egg, it broke through, and in a spill of blood, crawled out. Valerie sat up, and holding the grey mewling creature in front of her, smiled with relief. She cradled it against her chest and pressed her lips against it's forehead. Her teeth sank into the soft, newly-formed skull, as she scooped out a bite of its head, and sat there chewing, with a look of contentment on her face. Ah, the miracle of life.
